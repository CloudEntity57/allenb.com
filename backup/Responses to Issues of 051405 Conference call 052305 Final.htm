RESPONSES TO ISSUES IDENTIFIED IN THE MAY 17 CONFERENCE CALL

 

 

 

Please note that no legal advice should be construed as being given to other limited partners and the following is for discussion only.  I have sought the counsel of an attorney for these responses.

 

Just a note before the responses begin:  Even though many Limited Partners have interests in one or more partnerships, each of these partnerships is an individual entity, and reporting of each should be only to the Partners of that entity.  This communication is for General information that may pertain to one or all of the Partnerships.

 

 

Following are some issues/questions that were brought up during the conference call:

 

 

1)       A clearer understanding of what you are asking consent for from the LPs in your 5/14 notice to the LPs.

 

 

Consent of the Limited Partners to engage an attorney for the restructuring and re-constituting of the Limited Partnership agreements to allow for a new General Partner.  Also a consensus of who that General Partner should be.

 

 

2)        Who will represent John F. Walsh, Jr., LLC?

 

 

The Executor of his estate.

 

 

 

3)       What changes, if any, will be in the reconstituted partnership agreements from the current agreements?

 

 

It is recommended to keep the changes to a minimum. It will be necessary to appoint the new General Partner and convert Mr. Walsh's General Partnership interest to that of a Limited Partner.

 

 

4)       Can the reconstituted partnership agreements have a sunset date to dissolve the partnerships and the ability to extend this sunset date by LP vote?

 

:

 

The current partnership agreements have sunset dates that some consider too far into the future.  The restructured and re-constituted agreements could have shorter term sunset dates, but these could interfere with the Partners choices to do tax deferred exchanges and force them to pay capital gains taxes.  On the conference call everyone agreed that our shared goal is to sell the properties within six to nine months if the circumstances are favorable or as soon as possible thereafter.  In my opinion all of our interests can be served by leaving them unchanged.

 

 

 

 

5)       Do you and your wife feel you can handle the duties of GP/Realtor/Mngmt?

 

Since there is no General Partner, there is at present no ability to change the management company.  Upon completion of this legal process, the new GP will have the option of using any management company that will be advantageous for the Partnerships.  Whether we will continue in the management role after the situation is sorted and stabilized will depend upon numerous factors, especially our ability to streamline management functions.

 

 

6)       Have you hired real estate partnership attorneys?

 

 

Prior to our conference call I had consulted with two law firms. According to both, when a new Partnership is formed, an attorney is hired by the General Partner and represents the General Partner.  The legal expenses are reimbursed by the Partnerships.  In this case, the Partnerships are not new, yet the expenses are still paid by the Partnerships.  Limited Partners should understand that the Attorney still represents the General Partner, and therefore all questions will need to be addressed through the GP.  After our telephone conference on May 17, 2005, I did engage an attorney to represent our LLC, Allen Brothers Management, which will be the new General Partner. 

 

 

7)       Have you hired accountants to prepare the partnership financials?

 

 

We have submitted some of the data to four CPAs and are currently in the process of a final selection. We will officially engage a firm that meets all our criteria as soon as possible.  As soon as we are made aware of other information that is needed, we will have to spend the needed time to sort through the stacks of papers in Johns office to retrieve what they require.

 

 

8)       When will you have partnership income statements and balance sheets showing all the liabilities for each partnership?

 

We are doing all we can to expedite this process. The speed of generating this information is one of the main criteria we are using for our CPA selection.

 

 

9)       Are all partnership liabilities verifiable with a clean paper trail?

 

Yes. Since we took over a little after the middle of March 2005, we have kept clear records, as allowed by our limited ability to function without a GP. It will be up to the CPA to determine the condition of the books before we took over.

 

 

10)   Will you press liens against John's estate if required?

 

The attorney will work with the Executor to obtain consent to convert John Walsh Jr.s General Partnership interests to that of a Limited Partner.  If it is determined that he had done something wrong as General Partner, there are remedies available.

 

 

 

11)    What is the current fiscal and physical condition of the partnerships?

 

 

The fiscal condition of the partnerships has not been fully determined and will not be until the CPA completes the analyses.  The gross revenues have been increasing since we took over. The physical condition of the properties varies.  The most common problems are roofs, air conditioning, and siding in need of repair or replacement.  We have been performing needed maintenance to ready the units for rental and the properties for sale.

 

 

 

12)   What partnerships have prepayment penalties and what are the terms of the prepayment penalties?

 

 

 

The attorney has the deeds of trust and is in the process of obtaining the promissory notes on each respective loan to review. Some of the deeds of trust have transfer fees and require lender approval for an assumption.  The Partnerships that have prepayment penalties will be notified separately about these terms, when we know them fully.

 

 

 

13)   Is it legal to fax to the Texas Secretary of State a form indicating that we have a newly appointed General Partner before beginning and/or completing the attendant legal process of restructuring and re-constituting?

 

 

 

No, the General Partner must first be authorized to act under the Agreements of Limited Partnership.
