The services sector accounts for more than a fifth of Houston’s Gross Area Product.

Services represents more than a fifth of Houston CMSA Gross Area Product, followed by trade, finance/insurance/real estate and transportation/ communications/utilities, each with roughly a seventh.

The Perryman Group estimates Houston CMSA Gross Area Product for 2002 at $239.5 billion, up 4.06 percent from 2001. In constant (i.e., net of inflation) dollars, Houston's GAP is expected to have risen 2.59 percent in 2002.

Mining (which in Houston is almost entirely oil and gas exploration and production) accounts for 11.1 percent of Houston's GAP, down sharply from 21 percent as recently as 1985. The reduced role of oil and gas in Houston's GAP reflects the rapid growth of such other sectors as engineering services, health services and manufacturing.

Nineteen companies on the Fortune 500 list are headquartered in Houston.
Houston ranks fifth among metro areas (MSAs and CMSAs) in the number of Fortune 500 headquarters, behind New York, Chicago, San Francisco and Los Angeles. Many other Fortune 500 companies maintain U.S. administrative headquarters in Houston.
Of the world's 100 largest non-U.S.-based corporations, at least 49 have nonretail operations in Houston.
In mid-2002, Dun & Bradstreet reported 29,626 Houston-area (CMSA) business firms with annual sales of $1 million or more.

Seventy-seven foreign governments have representation in Houston through consular offices. Many also maintain trade offices here.
Houston consistently ranks as one of the top five cities in the nation in the number of consular offices.
Forty international chambers of commerce and trade associations have established themselves in Houston. Houston's foreign business and international community are also supported by more than 80 private business and cultural organizations.
Houston has 14 sister-city relationships promoting business and investment opportunities across four continents: Australia (1), Asia (5), Europe (7) and South America (1).
Twenty-two foreign banks representing 11 nations operate in Houston and provide financial assistance to the international community.
