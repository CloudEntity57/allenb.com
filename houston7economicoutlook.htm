HOUSTON, TEXAS

 

MARKET HIGHLIGHTS

Economy: Recovered with Job Growth

Houston has completed the recovery and is now in expansion mode.

Recession Snapshot: The recession hit Houston September ’08. The region lost 152,800 jobs through January ’10. Houston began to recoup jobs starting in February that year. By October ’11, the region had gained 153,000 jobs, or 101.1 percent of what it lost in the recession. Houston added more than 65,000 jobs between August 2010 and August 2011 alone.

The Greater Houston Partnership’s reports that  the10-county Houston metro area added 95,400 jobs in 2012 and are forecasting an additional 76,000 for 2013.

Economy benefits from high energy prices

The local economy continues to benefit from high energy prices, driving employment growth in the construction and mining sector which has risen 2.2% from a year ago. The professional and business services sector has also been driving growth with employment expanding 4% from its year-ago level. Unemployment in the metro is lower than the national average measuring 8.4% in the most recent month.

We expect the strength in this metro to continue given its low costs of doing business and the continued strength in the energy complex.

The Greater Houston Partnership’s forecast calls for the10-county Houston metro area to add 76,000 jobs in ’13.  Employment will grow in all sectors, with construction, health care, and leisure and hospitality turning in the strongest performances. 2013 should end with more tha 2.8 million jobs, a milestone for the region.

European Economic crisis insignificant: What happens in Europe is important to Houston, but it doesn’t determine our fate.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Population Increases

 

 

Population in the Houston region grew 26.1 percent from the 2000 census to the 2010 census.

 

 

Low taxes for individuals and business

 

 

Houston’s tax structure makes it a low cost center for doing business.

 

 

Houston has one of the lowest per-family tax burdens among major U.S. cities, according to an annual comparison issued by the government of the District of Columbia.

 

 

Educated labor force

 

 

Houston’s ready supply of recent college graduates in assorted business fields continues to strengthen the large talent pool available in the region.

 

 

Quality of life and year-round friendly climate

 

 

Houston brings world-class culture to residents and visitors alike. From the finest museums, symphonies and operas to its libraries, Houston’s cultural scene is well known for its breadth and diversity.

 

 

Climate friendly for outdoor recreational and construction activities. Houston has an average annual temperature of 70.6 degrees. With mild temperatures, Houstonians enjoy year-round outdoor activities.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

TEXAS

 


The Lone Star is shining bright.


The Texas real estate market is one of the few bright spots in the entire country. In fact, the Texas market has to be the brightest star still shining. First, we’ve got strong population growth, with over 2 million more Texans now than we had at the turn of the century. Second, our job growth is double the national average and likely to stay that way in 2013. Third, Texas is the place to live if you don’t like paying taxes. Not many states have a lower overall tax burden than we do. Fourth, Texas always ranks at or near the top of favorite places to relocate. Fifth, our housing is affordable and so is our lifestyle. It makes it easier for firms to attract workers when they can actually buy a home, too! And if those five factors aren’t enough, we still have the best Mexican food on the planet.

Texas outpaces nation

Despite the doom-and-gloom reports on the national scene, overall expect 2013 to be another busy year for residential and commercial real estate professionals south of the Red River.
